An entire book that is written in the perspective of Death is certain to keep your attention. “The Book Thief” was also the type of book that was just loaded knowledge and lessons to learn. Zusak constantly had readers look through different perspectives which allowed them to learn twice the information. Whenever a book has so much to offer like that, I tend to really find a love for it. But “The Book Thief” is really unique, and I have decided it made my top ten list of favorite books.

The majority opinion on "The Great Gatsby" by F. Scott Fitzgerald is that people don't really care for it because they do not understand the point he is trying to convey. But if you really dig deeper, beyond the words, you'll see that he is teaching us a lot in a few short pages.

This book is about the struggles of a girl in Afghanistan, who is given to an older man, for marriage. He treats her badly, but eventually they have children together and the young mom and children get away from the abusive environment, to make a better life for themselves. I really like books that show people getting themselves out of hardships because that is always something you can learn from others.

9 mins

"Life of Pi" is a book about hardships too, although the hardships faced by the protagonist are very different than most people. One of the reasons include the fact that his family owns a zoo, and they have to transport all of the animals by boat. They run into a storm and have to go through a lot of things just to survive. The things they face include hunger, wild animal fights, and murder. But the ending of the story allows you to be able to decide what actually happened, which is unique and why I liked it so much.

9 mins

This book is a very sad book. I typically stay away from really depressing books because there is enough sadness already. But, with the reoccurring theme here, I like books that tell the story of someone finding their way out of the difficult aspects of life. This book is also something I can relate to, in a way, which is a quality in a book that makes me like it so much.

9 mins

"Extremely Loud and Incredibly Close" is another book with an interesting perspective. The boy who leads the story is on the Spectrum and he is also very intelligent. He pretends to be a detective, searching all of New York City, to figure out some missing information about his dad, that has died. While it is an emotional adventure, it is also a unique story that I had trouble putting down.

9 mins

"I Know Why the Caged Bird Sings" is a coming-of-age novel that I had to read the summer before my eighth grade year. It is a novel I wished I waited to read until I was older to fully understand the things that happened. But when I read it, I still got a lot out of it. It had a lot of lessons and was, yet another story, about a young girl getting out of a tough situation, but looking back on it to see how it shaped her into a better human being. These are stories that help me learn a thing or two or ten about life, to make me into a better human being.

9 mins

"The Curious Incident of the Dog in the Night-Time" has a similar story line to "Extremely Loud and Incredibly Close" by Jonathan Safran Foer". This story is also written from the perspective of a boy who is on the spectrum. He has Asperger's Syndrome, where he pays a lot of attention to details, which is sometimes his downfall. But at the same time, it is his personality and he is a fantastic human being. He cares a lot about a dog in his neighborhood, that died, and he wants to figure out how it happened. While he does, he leads the readers on a wild adventure, making it difficult to ever stop reading as well.

9 mins
